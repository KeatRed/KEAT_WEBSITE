Renault Group

Renault is a French automobile manufacturer and part of the Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi Alliance.

My work site in Guyancourt specialized in vehicle and powertrain design, procurement,

and various central management departments.

My mission

The mission I carried out within the Renault Group as a software developer consultant involved enhancing and debugging various applications managed by the SIPMECA team, which is responsible for developing applications that monitor the production of electric and thermal engines on factory production lines.

From a business perspective, Renault had several production flow management software solutions, including SERMS , a software dedicated to tracking defects in produced components, and SIPIHM, an interface designed to manage manufacturing orders and monitor the status of automated systems. I was also tasked with contributing to the development of the future replacement for these systems, called IP4, which represented a web-based version of all the applications developed by Renault over the past 20 years. This web-based version centralized multiple applications and was accessible via mobile terminals, allowing operators to scan components using QR codes, thereby eliminating the need for manual text entry.

Thales AVS

Thales AVS France is a subsidiary of the Thales Group specializing in the design and manufacturing of

radiofrequency and microwave sources for professional applications.

So everything that makes up the payload of telecommunications satellites,

as well as components used in radars and missile seekers.

My mission

To ensure the proper production of their main products—traveling wave tubes, magnetrons, and klystrons—Thales operators use the well-known SAP software, supported by various applications on which I had the opportunity to work. Working within a team of scientists and being the sole developer in the team, I enjoyed both freedom in terms of development and significant skill growth in the industrial field (aerospace and space), thanks to the domain expertise of my colleagues.
These applications were primarily developed in :
      Back-End:
    • PHP
    • SQL(MySQL)
      Front-End:
    • JavaScript
    • HTML/CSS
    • Jquery
      Data format:
    • XML
      Managed using:
    • GitLab
    • Agile Scrum

From a business perspective, the synoptic application was designed to display exploded views of traveling wave tubes through binary trees, carefully detailing the parent and child components of each part to trace their origin, location, and manufacturing stage. The purpose of this was to calculate the start and end dates of production. One of the applications then allows for the creation of an acceptance test report using the information provided by the synoptic tool. This document is subsequently delivered to the client as proof that the product meets the criteria defined in the catalog.
